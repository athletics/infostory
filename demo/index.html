Creating The New Newsroom: The Forbes Model For Journalism In The Digital Age

Jock Whitney, one of the last publishers of the late, great New York Herald Tribune, said in the 1960s that news is more than what happens. He was referring to the insights, passion and life brought to events of the day by the Trib's murderer's row of reporters -- Tom Wolfe, Jimmy Breslin, Gail Sheehy, Judith Christ, Martin Mayer and many others.The Trib took journalism's essential mission to inform to an entirely new level by tapping the skills of its writers to observe, interpret and select, with all their biases. Taken together, the paper created a daily reading experience like no other.

In today's digital world, new product experiences for news can help inform consumers just like stories do. Then, they can facilitate one-on-one connections between all the participants -- journalists, readers and marketers. Two years ago, a group of product people, UI and UX designers, an art team and technologist set out to build such a product based on self-publishing, sharing, following and social interaction.

Today, with 1,000 writers, 400 posts a day, 33 million monthly unique visitors and a growing forum, we're on our way. There's much more to do, and we have a product road map that stretches till the end of 2013. For now, here's a look at what we've done so far, with my rollover remarks and observations about each one.
